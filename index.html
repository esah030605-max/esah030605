Industrial Scenario Case Study

Valorizing Sarawak Cultivar (Ananas comosus) Pineapple Waste in Johor, Malaysia

Malaysia generates over 250,000 metric tons of agricultural pineapple waste annually, particularly from the processing and canning hubs in Johor and Selangor. This interactive calculator simulates the bioconversion of liquid pineapple peel hydrolysate into single-cell protein or bio-ethanol by cultivating the yeast strain Saccharomyces cerevisiae in a stirred-tank batch bioreactor.

As an Agro Product Technology engineer, you cannot simply measure average growth; you must utilize the first derivative f'(t) (instantaneous growth rate) to pinpoint exactly when metabolic heat peaks, when dissolved oxygen is consumed fastest, and the optimal timestamp to stop fermentation to maximize efficiency and minimize utility costs.

Key Learning Objective

Understand how a logistic growth function's mathematical derivative represents physical biological phenomena (biomass output rate in grams per liter per hour).
